Data-Efficient Multimodal Training Strategies

  1. Home
  2. Work Package 1

Development of innovative approaches for data-efficient training of multimodel models, with a focus on time series and image data

This project aims to develop data-efficient multimodal training strategies for self-supervised learning, with a special focus on time series and images. The main goals here are:

  • Data-Efficiency: Strategies to reduce the needed amount of resources and data.
  • Multimodality: Strategies use the synergy between data from different sources.
  • Temporal Information: Strategies are considering patterns and trends in data over time.

Current large neutral networks require extensive datasets for training and computational resources. This poses a challenge in scenarios where resources such as time, money, and access to computational infrastructure are limited. Training strategies that reduce the amount of data and computational power needed to create a model would be beneficial in such scenarios. The goal of implementing the multimodal aspect in the training strategies is to allow models to enhance their performance by taking into account the complex information hidden not only in each modality, but also in the relations between different modalities. The planned research can be split into four distinct phases:

  • Cross-Modal Self-Supervised Contrastive Learning
  • Coreset Subset Selection Strategies
  • Data-Centric Curriculum Learning
  • Reinforcement Learning for Sample Selection
