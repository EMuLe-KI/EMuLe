Enhancing Multimodal Learning: Efficient, Adaptable, and Robust Model Training Strategies

This work package focuses on improving how multimodal models (which process different types of data, such as text, images, and time-series data) learn and perform. The goal is to enhance their efficiency and adaptability, making them better at both general-purpose tasks and specialized applications. A key challenge is reducing dependence on perfectly matched multimodal data during training and instead developing methods to train models using weakly aligned or even unpaired data. Additionally, the project will work on fine-grained learning to help models better differentiate between similar items, particularly when dealing with complex multimodal relationships. It will also explore domain-specific training techniques to ensure models capture specialized knowledge without losing generalizability. Another major focus is refining how different types of data interact within the model, using improved attention mechanisms to process and integrate information more effectively. Finally, the package aims to make these models more robust and reliable by using strategies such as adversarial training and advanced loss functions, ensuring they perform well even in unpredictable conditions.

The planned research can be split into four distinct phases:

  • Improve Training with Weakly Aligned or Unpaired Data.
  • Enhance Fine-Grained Recognition.
  • Develop Domain-Specific Training Approaches.
  • Improve How Models Process and Integrate Different Data Types.
