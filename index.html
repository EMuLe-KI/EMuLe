    Summary

    The rapid advancements in large neural networks, including Transformers and Large Language Models, have revolutionized machine learning but come with high demands for data and computational resources. This challenge is even more critical in multimodal learning, where memory and efficiency are essential. While most studies focus on image and text modalities, time series data remains an underexplored yet valuable source for applications like intelligent manufacturing, energy management, and healthcare.

    Processing multimodal data efficiently is especially challenging when data resources are limited, computational access is restricted, and cost and time constraints exist. Our aim is to develop data-efficient and model-efficient deep learning frameworks that enhance multimodal learning, particularly integrating images, time series, and other modalities, to optimize performance in resource-constrained environments.

    Our primary research questions are:

    • How can we optimize data-efficient training techniques to minimize the data volume required for training Transformers and large neural networks while maintaining model performance?
